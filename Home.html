The field of Sign Language Interpreting can be confusing, so this website is here to lay out some answers as simply as possible and to have them all in one place. So whether you are looking to request an interpreter, are already working with one, or might want to become one yourself, I hope this helps.


Vocabulary:

Deaf
Deaf: a broad term used for people who can not hear or have some level of hearing loss. On this website I will be using the word Deaf (with a capital D) as an umbrella term to include people of many different identities who may or may not identify with Deaf as a label (just to keep things simple). These identities include
  • Deaf (capital D) - people who identify culturally with the Deaf community
  • deaf (lowercase d) - people who are physically deaf but not culturally
  • Hard of Hearing - people who have lost some hearing and may or may not identify as culturally Deaf
  • DeafBlind - people who have lost some or all of both their hearing and vision
  • Late-deafened - people who lost their hearing later in life
Hearing
Hearing: people who can hear/are not Deaf.
ASL
American Sign Lanugage(ASL): a language that uses the hands, body and facial expressions to communicate instead of speech. ASL is used by Deaf people in the United States. Other countries have other sign languages.
